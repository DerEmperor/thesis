\chapter{Introduction}
\label{chapter:introduction}

In the era of "Big Data," the ability to derive actionable insights from massive datasets is a primary driver of innovation across industries, from healthcare analytics to financial modeling. However, the unchecked accumulation of data presents a profound conflict: organizations are legally and ethically obligated to protect the privacy of individuals—under frameworks such as the \ac{GDPR} and evolving global standards—while simultaneously needing to utilize that same data to train machine learning models and perform complex statistical analyses.


The market for data collection and processing is huge. Companies, law enforcement and even your employer can find great use in information about their customer, potential criminal or employee. Advertisements are more effective, if they are only served to future customers. Dynamic Pricing tries to set your personal price as high as possible to maximize profits. Your bill might be influenced by your income, spending behavior, interest in the product category or nationality. This may even continue at your job. Bosses want the most productive workers, which is a difficult metric to measure. This incentives enterprises to provide tools, that can quantify how valuable an employee is.
